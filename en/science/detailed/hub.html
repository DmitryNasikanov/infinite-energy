TL;DR

  • Goal: Receive energy from the Dyson Swarm and transmit to Earth
  • Solution: Lunar Solar Power Station (LSP) ‚Äî 20-40 stations on the Moon‚Äôs limbs
  • Efficiency: 18% (from Swarm to Earth)
  • Transmission: Microwaves through clouds, global rectenna network
  • Alternative (rejected): Orbital Hub at L1 ‚Äî 10% efficiency, 7,900 km¬≤ radiators

Concept

The mirror swarm in Mercury‚Äôs orbit produces petawatts of energy. But this energy is concentrated sunlight. It needs to be converted to a form suitable for transmission to Earth.

Concept by David Criswell: placing receivers on the Moon‚Äôs surface with microwave power transmission.

Geometry: Why Limbs?

The Moon is tidally locked ‚Äî one side always faces Earth. This creates a problem:

Position on Moon Sees Swarm? Sees Earth?
Near side Partially Yes
Far side Yes No
Limbs (edges) Yes Yes

Solution: 20-40 stations on the eastern and western limbs of the Moon.

Continuity of Operation

Stations on both limbs ensure continuous operation throughout the lunar month.

Receiving Energy from the Swarm

Mirror Clusters

Swarm mirrors are organized into local clusters of 1,000-10,000 units. Each cluster:

  • Synchronizes internally (delay ~0.3 ms)
  • Directs reflected light to designated LSP station
  • Forms a virtual antenna ~100 km in diameter

Global Rectenna Network

The Moon is visible ~12 hours from any point on Earth. Solution ‚Äî global rectenna network:

00:00 Pacific Ocean Australia Asia

Principle: Rectenna receives ‚Üí HVDC cables transmit to global grid ‚Üí consumers receive continuously.

Buffering: 1-2 hours of batteries smooth transitions between rectennas.

Advantage over solar: The Moon is visible day and night ‚Äî easier to ensure continuity.

Construction

A rectenna is an array of dipoles converting microwave radiation to electricity:

  1. Microwaves from LSP hit dipoles
  2. Dipoles generate alternating current
  3. Rectifiers convert to direct current
  4. HVDC transmits to grid

Rectenna efficiency: ~85%

System Components

The Hub Station consists of three main subsystems working as unified infrastructure:

Lunar LSP Stations

  • Quantity: 20-40 stations on Moon‚Äôs limbs
  • Photovoltaic area: ~10 km¬≤ per station (total ~300 km¬≤)
  • Materials: Multi-junction photocells from lunar silicon (Si), aluminum (Al) frames and supports
  • Production: Local, from lunar regolith

Microwave Transmitters

  • Location: On lunar LSP stations
  • Technology: Klystrons with 90% efficiency
  • Function: Convert electricity to microwaves for Earth transmission

Earth Rectennas

  • Quantity: ~100 stations worldwide
  • Size: ~100 km¬≤ each (10√ó10 km), total ~10,000 km¬≤ receiving area
  • Materials: Dipole arrays from aluminum (Al) or copper (Cu), silicon (Si) rectifier diodes ‚Äî Earth production
  • Function: Receive microwaves and convert to direct current (HVDC)

Energy Distribution

All Swarm energy is first received at lunar LSP stations, then distributed:

Stage Recipient Power % of Swarm
1. Reception Moon (LSP) ~20 PW 100%
‚Ü≥ 2a. Transmission Earth (rectennas) 1 PW 5%
‚Ü≥ 2b. Transmission Mars (future) 1-5 PW 5-25%
‚Ü≥ 2c. Local Moon (furnaces) ~0.5 PW ~2.5%
‚Ü≥ 2d. Buffer Moon (reserve) ~13-17 PW ~65-85%

Details of lunar usage: - Furnaces: Melting regolith in solar furnaces, MRE electrolysis, materials production - Reserve: NaS batteries for peak load smoothing, future production expansion

Why not all to Earth? 1 PW = 1000 TW ‚Äî this is 50 times global consumption (20 TW). It‚Äôs enough for complete decarbonization, synthetic fuels, and industrial growth. For Mars terraforming, energy is directed straight to Mars (1-5 PW), bypassing Earth. The rest is reserve for space expansion.

Alternative Architectures

Orbital Hub at L1

WarningConcept Rejected

Analysis showed economic infeasibility: 10% efficiency vs 18% for LSP, need to build 7,900 km¬≤ radiators in space.

Idea: Station at L1 point (Earth-Moon, 326,000 km from Earth) receives light from Swarm and transmits by laser to Earth.

Problem Hub (L1) LSP (Moon)
Efficiency 10% 18%
Radiators 7,900 km¬≤ in space 0 (ground)
Structure mass 15 million t in orbit ~178,000 t on surface
Construction In microgravity With gravity
Resources Delivery from Moon In-situ

Why such large radiators? At 35% efficiency at light‚Üílaser stage, about 65% of energy becomes heat. In vacuum, the only way to dump heat is radiation. At 1000K a radiator emits ~57 kW/m¬≤. For 0.45 PW, 7,900 km¬≤ area is needed.
