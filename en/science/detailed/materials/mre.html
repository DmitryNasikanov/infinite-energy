Regolith Feed to Furnace

Between the flow splitter and the solar furnace is a vibrating feeder with dosing system:

  1. Regolith flows into a receiving hopper above the furnace
  2. Vibration drive ensures uniform feed rate
  3. Gate valve regulates feed speed
  4. Regolith drops into the furnace crucible through the loading port

Feed is batch-based: load 500 kg ‚Üí melt ‚Üí transfer to MRE cell ‚Üí load next batch.

Solar Furnace

Principle: Concentration of sunlight by mirrors into a single point. On Mercury, solar flux is ~10 kW/m¬≤ ‚Äî 7 times greater than on Earth.

Why not an electric furnace?

Electricity on Mercury is precious ‚Äî it‚Äôs needed for electrolysis, motors, electronics. Sunlight is free and abundant. Mirrors focus 10 MW of thermal energy directly into the crucible, bypassing the light‚Üíelectricity‚Üíheat conversion.

An array of flat mirrors directs light to a parabolic reflector. The reflector focuses the beam into the crucible containing regolith.

Safety principle: Mirrors reflect light (operating T <100¬∞C), they don‚Äôt absorb it. All thermal energy is concentrated in the MgO crucible¬π (operating T 1500‚Äì2000¬∞C, melting point = 2852¬∞C ‚Äî margin of 852¬∞C). The rest of the structure remains cold (<200¬∞C).

¬π Magnite (MgO) is produced from local magnesium in regolith (slag distillation, 8% content, ~48 t/day).

Advantages of vacuum: - No convection ‚Äî heat is not carried away by air - No oxidation ‚Äî crucible doesn‚Äôt burn externally - No atmospheric absorption ‚Äî all light reaches the target

Note: These are ground-based concentrator mirrors on Mercury‚Äôs surface, not to be confused with the orbital Dyson Swarm. The first mirror set (~1 t, Kapton) is delivered from Earth, then produced locally from aluminum. See Production.

Furnace role: The solar furnace only melts regolith. Electrolysis and oxygen collection occur in a separate enclosed MRE cell, where melt is transferred via MHD channel.

Molten Regolith Electrolysis (MRE)

Principle: Passing current through molten regolith. Metals are deposited at the cathode, oxygen is released at the anode.

Oxygen Collection Principle

  1. Oxygen is released at anodes (O‚ÇÇ bubbles rise through melt)
  2. Gas collects in hood above melt surface
  3. Pipe channels O‚ÇÇ to cryogenic compressor
  4. Liquefied O‚ÇÇ is stored in insulated tanks (‚àí183¬∞C)

Important: The solar furnace is open at the top for the solar beam and only melts regolith. Oxygen is released in the enclosed MRE cell, where melt is transferred via MHD channel.

Reduction order (by Gibbs energy): Cr ‚Üí Mn ‚Üí Fe ‚Üí Si ‚Üí Ti ‚Üí Al ‚Üí Mg ‚Üí Ca. Metals with low energy (Fe, Mn) are reduced first and sink. Metals on the right (Al, Mg) float into slag.

Note on anodes: Anodes are consumable ‚Äî oxygen gradually oxidizes the material. Iridium anodes last ~6 months. This is the only component requiring regular import from Earth (mass ~10 kg/year per cell).

MHD Valve (Bottom Fe/Si Drain)

On an automated factory without personnel, an MHD valve is used ‚Äî the same principle as an MHD pump, but in reverse.

Parameter Description
Principle Magnetic field holds conducting melt
Closed Field creates back-pressure ‚Äî melt doesn‚Äôt flow
Open Field switched off ‚Äî melt flows under gravity
Regulation Field strength = flow rate
Materials Al coils, MgO ceramic channel ‚Äî all local

MHD valve advantages:

  • No moving parts in contact with melt
  • No wear ‚Äî magnetic field doesn‚Äôt wear out
  • Precise flow control (from drops to stream)
  • Instant open/close
  • Works at 1500¬∞C (coils are external, conductively cooled)
