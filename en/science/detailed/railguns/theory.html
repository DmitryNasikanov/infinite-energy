  • Physics: Electromagnetic acceleration to 5 km/s over 1 km track (1275g, 0.4 sec)*
  • Energy: 4.7 GJ per launch (115 kg container = 100 kg mirror)

Basic Parameters

Launch Physics

The Mercurian Mass Driver is an electromagnetic launcher that accelerates a 115 kg container with a mirror to escape velocity from Mercury.

Parameter Value
Mercury escape velocity 4.3 km/s
Target velocity 5 km/s (16% margin)
Track length 1 km (base configuration)*
Acceleration 12,500 m/s¬≤ (1275g)
Acceleration time ~0.4 sec

*When reducing load: 2 km (637g) or 3 km (425g). All options have 20x+ margin vs proven 25,000g.

Track Length Options

The base configuration (1 km) is chosen for maximum construction speed. If reducing load is needed, the track can be extended:

Parameter 1 km (base) 2 km 3 km
Acceleration 1275g 637g 425g
Acceleration time 0.4 sec 0.8 sec 1.2 sec
MD mass ~450 t ~875 t ~1300 t
Construction time ~25 days ~40 days ~60 days

Peak power 11.8 GW 5.9 GW 3.9 GW

Choice finalized during detailed design considering: - Lunar prototype test results (~640g) - Actual packaging survivability at high g - Available production capacity

Why 5 km/s instead of 4.3 km/s?

  • 4.3 km/s is the theoretical minimum to escape the gravity well
  • +0.7 km/s margin for trajectory correction, dispersion losses, maneuvering in the Swarm

Cryo-Aluminum

Problem: No Copper on Mercury

Mercury regolith contains 0.001% copper ‚Äî virtually zero concentration. Importing 1000 tons of copper for one Mass Driver is economically infeasible.

Material Conductivity at +20C Conductivity at -180C
Copper (Cu) 100% (58 MS/m) 400%
Aluminum (Al) 60% (35 MS/m) 360%

Conclusion: At -180C, aluminum reaches 360% of room temperature conductivity, which is 6√ó higher than normal aluminum.

Solution: Stationary Placement in Shadow

Mass Drivers are placed in stationary positions optimized by coordinates (latitude, longitude) for maximum time in shadow. Mercury‚Äôs dark side has -180C ‚Äî aluminum coils are naturally cooled:

  1. Mass Driver coils ‚Äî aluminum (local production), naturally cryogenic in shadow
  2. Solar panels ‚Äî nearby, on the sunlit side
  3. NaK loop ‚Äî heat removal from launches (not for cryo-cooling coils)
Configuration Efficiency Conditions Import
Solar period (+20C) 25-30% Reduced effectiveness 0 t
Shadow period (-180C) 35-40% Natural cooling 0 t
Copper import 40-45% Simple ~1000 t/MD

Implications: - Efficiency 35-40% for most of the Mercurian day (same as room-temperature copper) - Zero import of materials for coils - NaK loop needed only for launch heat removal, not for cryo-cooling

Critical Issues

1. Heat Dissipation During Launch

Parameter Value
Energy per launch 4.7 GJ
Efficiency 40%
Heat per shot 2.8 GJ = 280 MW for 10 sec
Interval between shots 2-3 minutes

Problem: 280 MW of heat must be removed in 2-3 minutes. At 600 shots/day ‚Äî continuous heat dissipation ~40 MW.

Consequences: - Coil overheating ‚Üí efficiency drop ‚Üí more heat - Thermal expansion of rails ‚Üí projectile jamming - Coil insulation degradation

Solution: - In shadow period (-180C): large delta-T simplifies heat radiation ‚Äî radiators are more compact - NaK loop for launch heat removal (coils are already naturally cold) - In solar period: reduced firing rate or standby

Lunar Mass Driver

Purpose

Mass Driver technology testing before Mercury deployment. The Moon is a proving ground where mistakes are cheaper: communication 1.3 sec (vs 8-20 min), delivery 3 days (vs months).

Conclusion: The Lunar Mass Driver is comparable in length (0.5-1 km vs 1 km), with ~640g acceleration (~2x lower than Mercury) ‚Äî suitable for technology development before transitioning to the high-g Mercurian version.
