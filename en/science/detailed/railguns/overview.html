Three Stages of Mass Driver Production

Mass Driver production is divided into three sequential stages:

1. Element Production (Materials -> Components)

Finished materials (Al/Fe/Si) are transformed into Mass Driver components.

Three element production lines:

  • Rail Production - aluminum guides for acceleration
  • Coil Winding - electromagnetic windings (cryo-aluminum)
  • Tunnel Frame - 1 km steel frame

Materials: Al (500 t) + Fe (800 t) from regolith processing

2. Track Assembly (Components -> Mass Driver)

Components are assembled into a finished 1 km Mass Driver (base configuration).

Three final assembly lines:

  • Track Assembly - rail laying and coil mounting
  • Control Electronics - control systems and sensors
  • Testing - functionality verification

Assembly time: ~3-4 months for the first Mass Driver, ~1-2 months for subsequent units (experience curve)

3. Operations

After commissioning, the Mass Driver operates at 600 launches/day.

Parameter Value
Launch cycle 2-3 minutes
Launches per hour 20-30
Launches per day 600
Launches per year 219,000

Throughput: 600 x 116 kg = 70 tons of mirrors/day = 26,000 t/year

Material Balance

Localization: 98.5% by mass

Material production time (1 km): - Al: 170 t / 42 t/day = 4 days - Fe: 270 t / 18 t/day = 15 days

TOTAL (1 km): ~15-20 days for material accumulation + 5-7 days for assembly = ~25 days

*When extended to 3 km: ~60 days (45 days Fe + 15 days assembly).
