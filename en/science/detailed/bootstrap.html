First Factory Assembly (Bootstrap)

TL;DR

  • Critical checkpoint: Energy autonomy within 12-24 hours ‚Äî after this, assembly time is NOT critical
  • Total time: 7-32 days (depending on conditions), but post-checkpoint constraint is only Gen-1 lifespan (2-3 years)
  • Gen-1 batteries: Li-S (not Li-Ion!) ‚Äî operates down to -60¬∞C
  • In-flight charging: Panels on lander module (~50 m¬≤) charge robots during 3-4 month flight
  • Dome: Pre-assembled inflatable module from Earth (deployment in 4-8 hours)

Site Selection: Polar Crater

Critical: Bootstrap takes place at Mercury‚Äôs pole (Prokofiev crater, 85¬∞N), NOT at the equator. This fundamentally changes the thermal regime.

Why the Pole, Not the Equator?

Parameter Mercury Equator Pole (Prokofiev crater)
Day temperature +430¬∞C Peaks: ~+100¬∞C (low sun angle)
Night temperature -180¬∞C Crater floor: -150¬∞C (permanent)
Day/night cycle 176 Earth days No cycle ‚Äî eternal shadow + eternal light
Li-S compatibility (-60¬∞C‚Ä¶+60¬∞C) Impossible Yes (operation in shadow/terminator)

Crater Thermal Zones

A polar crater has three thermal zones:

  1. Peaks of eternal light (crater rims) ‚Äî permanently lit (~+100¬∞C), solar panels are placed here
  2. Terminator zone (slopes) ‚Äî variable lighting (-50¬∞C‚Ä¶+50¬∞C), Gen-1 robot work zone
  3. Eternal shadow (crater floor) ‚Äî permanent -150¬∞C to -180¬∞C, storage, cooling, water ice

Gen-1 operating mode: Robots operate in the terminator zone and on illuminated slopes. If overheating, they retreat to shadow for cooling. Li-S batteries (-60¬∞C‚Ä¶+60¬∞C) are fully compatible with this regime.

The 88-day ‚Äúnight‚Äù myth: This applies only to the equator. At the poles, ‚Äúpeaks of eternal light‚Äù are lit continuously (24/7/365). The 88-day night problem does not apply to the polar base.

Why This is a Unique Phase

Bootstrap is the only moment in the project when there are no local resources and nowhere to charge robots. After factory startup, standard production with self-replication begins.

Robots Gen-1 (Ti + Li-S) Gen-2 (Fe/Al + NaS)
Materials 100% imported 97% local
Dome Pre-assembled inflatable On-site assembly
Energy Limited by batteries Unlimited
Critical path Energy autonomy Throughput
Repair Spare parts limited Local manufacturing

Key insight: Until charging infrastructure is deployed, robots operate on batteries (15-24 hours). After checkpoint they can recharge ‚Üí further assembly time is constrained only by Gen-1 lifespan (2-3 years).

In-flight Charging

Problem

Flight to Mercury takes 3-4 months. How do robots arrive charged if batteries last only 15-24 hours?

Solution: Panels on Lander Module

The lander module is equipped with deployable solar panels for charging robots in flight:

Parameter Value
Panels on module ~50 m¬≤ GaAs
Power (near Mercury) ~180 kW (GaAs 35%)
Total battery capacity (56 robots) ~890 kWh
Full charge time ~5 hours

Flight Mode

  1. Launch: Robots charged to 100% (on Earth)
  2. Cruise phase (3-4 months): Panels deployed, trickle charge compensates self-discharge
  3. Li-S self-discharge: ~2-3%/month ‚Äî minimal losses
  4. Before landing: Final full charge
  5. Landing: Robots at 100% charge, ready for bootstrap

Li-S batteries are optimal for long-term storage in space: low self-discharge (~2-3%/month) and resistance to temperature variations. Detailed comparison with Li-Ion and NaS ‚Äî in Batteries section.

Pre-assembled Inflatable Dome

Inflatable module (10-12 t) deploys in 4-8 hours.

Analog: Bigelow Aerospace BEAM module on ISS (16 m¬≥, 1.4 t) ‚Äî operating successfully since 2016.

BEAM ‚Üí Helios scaling: BEAM is a rigid module with full life-support (1 atm, 87 kg/m¬≥). The Helios dome is a lightweight structure: shell only (no life support ‚Äî no humans), multi-layer composite instead of metal, 0.1 atm. Under these conditions, specific mass of ~1.3-1.6 kg/m¬≥ is realistic.

Deployment process:

  1. Unload module (Crab-Z √ó4, 1 hour)
  2. Position on site (Centaur-Z √ó6, 1 hour)
  3. Inflate with N‚ÇÇ (imported; subsequent domes use local O‚ÇÇ) (automatic, 30 min)
  4. Connect airlock (Centaur-Z √ó4, 1 hour)
  5. Leak check (30 min)
  6. Dome ready (4 hours)
