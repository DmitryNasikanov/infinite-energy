Moon

TL;DR

  • Role 1: Test site before Mercury (years 4-6)
  • Role 2: LSP stations for transmitting energy to Earth
  • Role 3: Backup site ‚Äî less risky alternative to Mercury
  • Advantage: 1.3 sec communication delay (vs 8-20 min to Mercury), mistakes are cheaper
  • Test MD: 0.5-1 km, 2.5 km/s ‚Äî technology validation

Why the Moon?

The Moon serves three roles in the project:

  1. Test site (years 4-6) ‚Äî technology validation before Mercury
  2. LSP stations (years 6+) ‚Äî receiving energy from the Swarm and transmitting to Earth
  3. Backup site ‚Äî less risky alternative to Mercury

Advantages of the Lunar Test Site

Parameter Moon Mercury
Communication delay 1.3 sec 8-20 min
Cargo delivery 3 days Months
Cost of mistakes Low High
Sample return Possible Difficult

Conclusion: Mistakes on the Moon are cheaper. Better to debug technologies here.

Test Site (Years 4-6)

Robots learn to work in space:

  • Mining: Mole-M excavators dig regolith
  • Smelting: MRE electrolysis for metal extraction
  • Construction: WAAM 3D printing, structure assembly
  • Energy: solar panel deployment

Experience transfers: Robots tested on Moon adapt for Mercury. Detailed comparison of conditions: Why Mercury?

LSP Stations

The Moon is a key element of the power bridge. Lunar Solar Power stations receive light from the Swarm and transmit energy to Earth via microwaves.

Transmission Scheme

Dyson Swarm ‚Üí Light to Moon ‚Üí PV stations ‚Üí Microwaves ‚Üí Earth (rectenna)

Location: Lunar Limbs

The Moon is tidally locked ‚Äî one side always faces Earth. Stations are placed on limbs (edges), where both the Swarm and Earth are visible.

20-40 stations on eastern and western limbs provide continuous transmission.

Learn more: Energy Receiving Hub ‚Äî efficiency, rectenna, microwave safety

Alternative to Mercury

The Moon is not only a testbed but also a backup path for the entire project.

Key tradeoff: Mercury is faster (~10 years), Moon is safer (~14 years, but cheaper mistakes). If critical failure on Mercury, production can be fully transferred to the Moon.
